# Project Status & Handoff — 2026-06-16

## What Is pixel-mixel?

A fully-destructible 2D side-scrolling platformer where the world is composed of 4×4px atomic chunks. Everything can be broken apart, and the pieces interact with physics.

## Where We Are

### Completed (Phase 0 + Phase 1 in progress)

**Core systems working:**
- Chunk grid (500×144 chunks, 3 bytes each, dense PackedByteArray)
- 5 biomes generating correctly (ocean shore, forest surface, forest lake, cave, desert)
- 10 terrain types (dirt, stone, water, grass, leaves, wood, sand, mushroom, cactus, grass_solid)
- Player as CharacterBody2D with move_and_slide (Godot native physics)
- Terrain collision generated from chunk grid (StaticBody2D with merged rect shapes)
- Blade attack: directional stab toward cursor, power-vs-toughness, spawns RigidBody2D chunks
- Parallax backgrounds per biome
- Sprite animation (idle/walk/attack states)
- Sand always LOOSE, pre-settled at generation
- Trees/grass passable (walk through), destructible
- Loose chunks fly as RigidBody2D when freed
- 62+ automated tests, snapshot baselines committed

**Architecture (ADR-013 — current state):**
- **Player & launched chunks:** Godot physics (CharacterBody2D, RigidBody2D)
- **Terrain collision:** StaticBody2D generated from grid, rebuilt per-region on destruction
- **Rendering:** Image-based (full grid → ImageTexture), dirty-region partial updates
- **World generation:** Grid-based pipeline (plugins → ChunkSimulator settles sand/water → done)
- **Runtime sim:** REMOVED for solids. Liquids currently static at runtime (issue #53 to fix)

## How We Got Here

### Phase 0 (complete)
Started from zero. Established chunk grid, generation pipeline, physics sim, renderer, test harness. Proved the concept: generate a world, simulate it to equilibrium, render it, walk through it.

### Phase 1 (in progress)
Added 5 biomes, 10 terrain types, destruction via blade attack, parallax backgrounds. Midway through, hit a wall with custom grid physics at runtime — manual AABB collision was fragile, chunks couldn't fly convincingly, frame spikes from re-rendering the whole world.

**Major refactor (ADR-013):** Switched runtime physics to Godot native. Player became CharacterBody2D, freed chunks became RigidBody2D. Kept grid sim for generation only. Performance improved dramatically for player movement and chunk launching.

**Current pain points fixed:**
- Sand slopes smoothly into ocean (sim runs at gen-time)
- Terrain collision from grid (per-region rebuild on destruction)
- Dirty-region rendering (only re-render what changed)
- Attack only rebuilds affected collision region, not whole world

**Current pain points remaining:**
- Water doesn't flow at runtime (#53)
- Freed chunks disappear instead of re-entering grid (#54)
- Loose chunks need individual borders (#55)

## Next Steps

1. **#53 — Water flow at runtime.** Dirty-region liquid sim. When terrain near water is destroyed, water fills the gap.
2. **#54 — Loose chunks return to grid.** RigidBody2D at rest → write back to grid → render + collision update.
3. **#55 — Loose chunk borders.** Individual black borders on all loose chunks in grid.

After those three, the physics feel should be significantly better — water reacts to destruction, chunks pile up permanently, everything has visual weight.
